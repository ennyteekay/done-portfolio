About me

My process

How I work

From understanding the problem to shipping with confidence, here's the framework I follow to build products that hold up under real-world use.

  1. 1

    Understand the problem first

    Gather user insights, stakeholder context, and data signals to clearly define the problem worth solving, before committing to a direction. I align teams around a shared understanding of the opportunity and its business impact.

  2. 2

    Research & frame the strategy

    Competitive analysis, market research, and user interviews inform the product strategy. I frame the problem tightly, define success metrics, and build the roadmap that connects user needs to business outcomes, combining PM rigour with the design lens that catches what specs can miss.

  3. 3

    Design, prototype & validate

    I translate strategy into user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes, then test with real users. My design fluency means I partner with designers as a collaborator, not a bottleneck, and catch UX issues before they reach engineering.

  4. 4

    Build, QA & ship with confidence

    I lead cross-functional execution through structured QA, UAT, and clear sign-off criteria. Nothing ships until it meets the quality bar, and I stay in the detail through triage, regression testing, and post-launch monitoring to ensure it holds up.
